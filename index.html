Ytterbium is a chemical element of the periodic table with the symbol Yb and atomic number 70. Ytterbium is a soft, silvery metallic element, a rare earth of the lanthanide series found in gadolinite, monazite, and xenotime. Ytterbium is sometimes associated with yttrium or other related elements and is used in some steels. Natural ytterbium is a mixture of seven stable isotopes.

Ytterbium was discovered in 1878 by Jean Charles Galissard de Marignac, who identified a new oxide while studying the mineral erbium. Later, in 1907, Georges Urbain separated that oxide into two elements: ytterbium and lutetium. Pure ytterbium metal was isolated in 1953. Like many rare earth elements, its discovery was gradual and complex, involving several scientists and chemical separation techniques.
